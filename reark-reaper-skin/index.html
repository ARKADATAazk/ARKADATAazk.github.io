Here’s how to awawa: Megata, the acid lead.

Here is the original sound. Some information we can gather already: The synth is mono, with just a little bit of reverb. It’s apparently a saw wave, with a lot of filtering to give it that organic sound, and some notes seems to glide into each other, much like someone would do when singing.

ARKADATA · Megata Kyojin Kuchiku Original

Now that I have a basic idea of what the sound consists of in terms of sound design. Let’s check the melody to make a copy as close as possible to the original.

I’ll use a piano for that purpose.

ARKADATA · SWN Megata PFver

Next, I’m gonna use one of my synths to get as close as possible to the original sound. Zebra from u-He has a lot of good filtering possibilities along with a good legato system. It should be able to do the job, though results may vary slightly from the original due to how differents synths can sound. 

Anyway, the goal here isn’t to achieve a 100% copy. It’s to understand a craft; we are learning a recipe to make many variations of that kind of sound.

Filter KeyFollow

Great way to add variation on our filter. Have its cutoff follow the current note.

I ended up using a lot of different filters with different levels of resonance, with the cutoff that follows an envelope. Thanks to how Zebra handle things, the filter does not reset when playing legato. 

A simple mono saw wave with a single voice does the job for the OSC. The LP Filters will do the rest.

What’s important is the glide and the filter’s motion. The thing with Megata’s synth is that it’s not always gliding/in legato. It’s humanized in a way, to give it some variation.

Wanted something that felt bold and determined as soon as the button was pressed. So I started with this idea by working on a bassline that could be reminiscent of Valorant’s soundtrack or even the Psyops theme from the League of Legends skin line.

#Process Video → Bassline & Tech

The bassline was made using multiple layers of u-He Diva, spatialized differently with a bunch of automation. Kicks and most of the other synths were made using u-He Zebra, sometimes rendered and reversed or cut in different ways to make it more organic.
Low/Midrange vox is a Zebra’s synth. Used my voice for the higher/more ‘breathy’ one.

#Process Video → Orchestral Ascension

Tried to compose something that could end on a VI VII i. Didn’t want it to sound too triumphant by playing a major I as the road to worlds is quite challenging.
Overall, the switch from a Dark Tech soundtrack to Orchestral really works well when the ascension starts, which is supposed to be more uplifting.
Added choirs to double down on the ‘epic’ challenge.
