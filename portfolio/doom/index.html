Viking Village

March 2021

Student project using the free Viking Village map from Unity’s assets to study Wwise.

This was a group project where we had to do the sound design and audio integration to bring a map to life. 

The map was overhauled and multiples scripts were coded in order to achieve our creative vision and try out different systems.

  • Tools:

    Unity / Wwise / Reaper / C#

Official Documentation

Read More

Fantastic project with a great team. In this I was responsible for handling the Unity session (game design and audio systems programming) and most of what was done on Wwise, acting as an audio and creative lead for a team of 7, with some sound design work as well.

It started off as an exercise to get us introduced to Wwise and Unity but we had a lot in mind and many audio systems we wanted to test out so it ended up being more than that, it was a research playground and the map changed a bit from its original state (many evenings were lost to Unity, slowly learning what it can achieve).

What sparked this project ascension to fantastic status was our teacher telling us:

“Yes of course you can sound design a dragon, even if it’s not on the map”

The initial goal was to take the map as-is, and sound design it as accurately as possible, discovering Wwise’s potential through Unity and honing our sound design skills. But our first idea was to make it more alive than it looked with that dragon, our ideas slowly evolved into a new vision for the map and as we worked on it we always needed new systems that had to be thought out and coded.

Here are some interesting challenges we faced.
